# Halava Consolidated Founder Agreement

> Last updated: March 9, 2026
> Status: Working draft (single-file version)

## 1. Founder Agreement

### 1.1 Purpose and Scope

This document is the single working draft for the founder relationship. It combines the core founder agreement, the proposed equity split, the IP assignment rules, and the vesting structure into one file so the commercial and operating terms can be reviewed together.

This draft is intended to make the founder relationship clear before final legal drafting. It is meant to answer four practical questions:

- who is responsible for what
- how ownership is earned
- who owns the work product created for Halava
- what happens to equity over time and on departure

### 1.2 Parties

- **Founder**: product, technical, and long-term operating lead
- **Co-founder**: incorporation, capital, finance/admin, and commercial execution lead

Both parties are intended to be treated as genuine founders. The structure is not meant to position the Founder only as a future employee or the Co-founder only as a passive investor. Each side is expected to contribute in a founder capacity.

### 1.3 Working Principles

- Both founders must act in the best interests of Halava.
- Equity and salary are separate concepts. Equity reflects long-term ownership. Salary reflects actual work performed.
- Ownership is earned by completed contribution, not by title alone.
- No founder should receive unearned ownership only because time has passed.
- Major economic or control changes require written approval by both founders.
- Stage `1` staffing decisions should be made by mutual discussion and then recorded in writing in a simple form such as message or meeting note.

### 1.4 Founder Responsibilities

The Founder is responsible for product and technical execution. This includes defining the product scope, setting the technical direction, building the product, and delivering the agreed launch scope.

Founder commitments:

- own product scope, technical architecture, and implementation
- manage day-to-day engineering execution and launch delivery
- work part-time until graduation, within the legally permitted scope
- after graduation, transition to full-time employee status in Halava once visa status permits
- prepare to become the long-term CEO after the agreed transition conditions are met

Stage `1` operating arrangement for the Founder:

- Stage `1` runs from March 2026 through August 2026
- founder-first execution is the default staffing model
- founder part-time salary is fixed at `JPY 1,500/hour` for approved working hours during Stage `1`
- if founder capacity is no longer sufficient to maintain the agreed milestone schedule, additional part-time support is added after mutual discussion and recorded in writing

The Stage `1` founder salary is intended as a modest operating expense for actual work done. It is not intended to replace equity and is not a disguised distribution.

### 1.5 Co-founder Responsibilities

The Co-founder is responsible for company setup, capital support, and business-side execution. This includes incorporation support, staged capital commitments, finance/admin setup, and commercial traction.

Co-founder commitments:

- support incorporation and early company operations
- complete the staged capital commitments described in this document
- support finance, accounting, legal coordination, and administration
- support customer pipeline, merchant relationships, and commercial execution
- discuss and agree any additional Stage `1` part-time staffing, with the decision recorded in writing

During Stage `1`, the Co-founder does not receive salary by default. The Co-founder's economic return during this phase is primarily through equity earn-in, unless both founders agree otherwise in writing.

### 1.6 Stage 1 Operating Rules

Stage `1` is the build-to-launch period. The working assumption is that the first `JPY 3M` is used to give Halava enough runway to build, prepare for launch, and retain any unused balance as company reserve.

The practical operating rules for Stage `1` are:

- founder handles the execution load first
- additional part-time support is added only when founder capacity is no longer sufficient
- any additional part-time support should be discussed by both founders in advance and recorded in writing by message or meeting note
- founder rate remains fixed through Stage `1`
- any change to founder salary rate is reviewed only at Stage `2` and requires written approval by both founders
- unused Stage `1` funds remain with Halava as company reserve after launch

### 1.7 Decision-Making and Reserved Matters

The following matters require written approval by both founders, unless the final legal documents later define an equivalent supermajority rule:

- new equity or ownership issuance
- changes to founder ownership
- founder compensation changes
- any change to the Stage `1` founder rate before Stage `2` review
- removal of a founder from executive authority
- debt above an agreed threshold
- sale of the company or major assets
- related-party transactions
- amendments affecting economics or control

### 1.8 Conduct, Confidentiality, and Records

Both founders must keep Halava confidential information confidential and must not use Halava assets, customer relationships, or company opportunities for personal or outside-business benefit without written approval.

Each founder must disclose outside interests that could create a conflict with Halava, especially where there is any possibility of channel conflict, resource conflict, or related-party transaction.

Halava should keep written records of:

- stage sign-offs
- capital contributions
- founder approvals
- ownership updates
- major company decisions affecting economics or control

### 1.9 Departure and Cooperation

If a founder leaves, is removed, or defaults, the founder must cooperate in a clean transition. This includes returning or transferring all company property, accounts, credentials, code, documents, and operating records without delay.

Departure economics are governed by the vesting and repurchase section below. Operational handover is separate from equity treatment and remains mandatory.

## 2. Equity Split

### 2.1 Structure Overview

The target long-term ownership structure is:

| Role | Target Ownership | Basis |
|------|------------------|-------|
| Founder | `55%` | product and technical delivery |
| Co-founder | `45%` | capital commitment and business-side execution |

This structure is based on contribution and risk allocation, not on a formal valuation exercise. The proposal assumes that the Founder contributes the core product build and technical leadership, while the Co-founder contributes capital, company setup, finance/admin support, and commercial execution.

### 2.2 Full Earn-In Principle

The `55/45` structure is not treated as fully owned on day one. It is fully earned only after both founders complete and sign off Stage `3`.

The intended balance at launch is that the Founder has completed `Stage 2` while the Co-founder has completed `Stage 1`. This reflects the fact that product and technical work is more front-loaded before launch, while capital deployment, company operations, and commercial execution continue more heavily after launch.

Until then:

- each founder owns only the percentage already earned through completed stages
- any remaining percentage stays unearned
- unearned equity does not transfer automatically
- unearned equity does not automatically move to the other founder

This is important because it avoids a mismatch where one founder stops contributing but still keeps ownership that was never actually earned.

### 2.3 Co-founder Capital Commitment

The Co-founder's staged capital commitment is:

| Stage | Capital Commitment | Intended Use |
|------|---------------------|--------------|
| `1` Formation / Build | `JPY 3M` | build-to-launch runway from March-August 2026 |
| `2` Launch / Early Operations | `+JPY 5M` | initial post-launch operating period |
| `3` Running Capital / Growth | `+JPY 7M` | ongoing operations and growth |

Unless both founders later agree otherwise in writing, these commitments are treated as founder funding tied to the earn-in structure and not as valuation-priced external investment rounds.

Stage `1` is expected to cover:

- founder part-time salary
- cloud and operating services needed for MVP build and launch readiness
- additional part-time support if founder capacity is no longer sufficient and both founders agree after discussion

If Stage `1` funds are not fully used by launch, the unused portion remains with Halava as company reserve.

The intended post-launch timing is:

- Co-founder `Stage 2` should be completed within `120 days` after launch
- Co-founder `Stage 3` should be completed within `12 months` after launch
- any remaining co-founder equity not earned by the end of that period remains unearned unless both founders agree otherwise in writing

### 2.4 Founder Earn-In Schedule (`55%`)

| Stage | Cumulative Founder Ownership | Detailed Milestone | Expected Completion Evidence |
|------|-------------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------|
| `1` | `20%` | product scope finalized, technical architecture defined, repository and engineering foundation ready, baseline authentication/onboarding implemented | approved scope note, architecture note, working repository, baseline demo |
| `2` | `45%` | core MVP features implemented and deployable, including the agreed marketplace and merchant workflow baseline, with launch-ready release completed | deployable staging build, feature walkthrough, test accounts, launch checklist, written sign-off |
| `3` | `55%` | post-launch execution completed, including `60-90` days of stable production operation, launch blockers resolved, monitoring/logging in active use, and agreed support/ops flow working | uptime review, blocker log, monitoring/logging proof, support/ops checklist, written sign-off |

The Founder earns ownership by delivery, not by intention. Commercial performance does not replace the Founder's delivery obligations, although commercial progress may be used as additional evidence of launch readiness if both founders agree.

The purpose of moving the Founder's final `10%` into a post-launch stage is to avoid a situation where the Founder is fully earned out at launch while the Co-founder is still carrying meaningful post-launch capital and commercial obligations.

### 2.5 Co-founder Earn-In Schedule (`45%`)

| Stage | Capital | Cumulative Co-founder Ownership | Detailed Milestone | Expected Completion Evidence |
|------|---------|----------------------------------|--------------------|------------------------------|
| `1` | `JPY 3M` | `20%` | incorporation support completed, initial capital funded, bank/accounting/legal setup completed, Stage `1` budget approved | registry records, funding proof, bank setup proof, accounting setup proof, written budget |
| `2` | `+JPY 5M` | `35%` | early operations funded, finance/admin process running, first `5` pilot merchants signed or onboarding, invoicing/expense process established, completed within `120 days` after launch | funding proof, merchant list, onboarding status, process note, written sign-off |
| `3` | `+JPY 7M` | `45%` | operating commitment maintained, operating budget funded, at least `10` active merchants or equivalent commercial validation, founder transition support in place, completed within `12 months` after launch | funding proof, operating budget, active merchant list, KPI summary, transition note, written sign-off |

Capital alone does not complete a stage. The related operational and commercial milestone for the same stage must also be completed.

### 2.6 Sign-Off Rules

- each stage is earned only after written confirmation by both founders
- no partial earn-in applies unless both founders agree in writing
- if a founder does not complete the contribution required for a stage, that stage ownership is not earned
- a completed stage cannot be retroactively changed by one founder alone
- if Co-founder `Stage 2` or `Stage 3` is not completed within the agreed post-launch timing, the corresponding unearned equity remains unearned unless both founders agree otherwise in writing

If the product roadmap or commercial assumptions change materially, the founders should update the stage definitions in writing before using the new standard for sign-off.

### 2.7 Salary and Equity Distinction

The Founder may receive Stage `1` part-time salary and still earn equity. That is not treated as double payment because the two items serve different functions:

- salary pays for actual work performed during the runway period
- equity rewards long-term founder contribution and risk

The Co-founder may have no Stage `1` salary by default and still earn equity because the Co-founder's economic contribution in that period is expected to come through capital commitment and company-building work.

## 3. IP Assignment

### 3.1 Assignment Principle

Everything created specifically for Halava should belong to Halava. This is necessary so the company, not an individual founder, controls the product, technology, brand, and operating materials.

Without a clear assignment rule, the company can face avoidable risk in fundraising, hiring, operations, or founder departure. This section is intended to prevent that problem.

### 3.2 What Must Be Assigned

The following categories of work product created for Halava are assigned to Halava:

- source code, repositories, scripts, and internal tools
- product specifications, flows, wireframes, mockups, and design files
- data models, schemas, architecture notes, and deployment configuration
- infrastructure configuration, cloud setup, observability setup, and security configuration
- names, marks, logos, branding, and visual assets created for Halava
- merchant onboarding materials, sales materials, pricing materials, and operating playbooks
- internal documentation, process documentation, and business materials

The rule applies regardless of whether the work was created by the Founder, the Co-founder, or an approved part-time contributor working for Halava.

### 3.3 Pre-Existing IP

Pre-existing IP is not automatically assigned. If either founder brings a pre-existing asset into Halava, the asset should be clearly listed and its treatment should be documented in writing.

Possible treatments are:

- assign it fully to Halava
- license it to Halava on agreed terms
- exclude it from Halava use

Working schedule for pre-existing IP:

| Founder | Asset | Treatment |
|---------|-------|-----------|
| `TBD` | `TBD` | assign / license / exclude |

If an asset is not listed as pre-existing, the working assumption should be that work created for Halava is company IP.

### 3.4 Control of Company Assets and Accounts

Halava must be able to control and access its own critical systems. No founder should keep exclusive practical control over a system that the company depends on.

This includes:

- source repositories
- cloud accounts
- domains and DNS
- analytics and monitoring tools
- payment tools
- merchant-facing operational tools
- shared documents and records

At least one backup access path should exist for critical systems, and the company should be able to continue operating if one founder becomes unavailable.

### 3.5 Confidential Information

The following should be treated as Halava confidential information:

- source code
- credentials and security information
- customer and merchant lists
- business pipeline information
- financial data
- product plans and internal roadmap material
- pricing and operating information

Neither founder may disclose or reuse this information outside Halava except with written approval or where legally required.

### 3.6 Third-Party Materials and Outside-Business Assets

Any material third-party dependency used in Halava should be documented to a practical level, especially where the dependency creates licensing cost, recurring payment obligations, or legal restrictions.

No founder should introduce:

- third-party licensed assets that create ongoing payment or legal exposure without disclosure
- proprietary assets from another business without prior written approval
- usage terms that materially restrict Halava's freedom to operate

This rule is especially important where a founder has outside business activities that could overlap with Halava.

### 3.7 Departure Rule for IP and Access

On departure, a founder must immediately transfer or return:

- credentials
- repositories
- code
- company records
- devices or storage containing company material
- any other item needed for continued operation

Departure does not create a right to retain Halava IP or continue controlling Halava systems.

## 4. Vesting Schedule

### 4.1 Core Principle

Earn-in and vesting are separate.

Earn-in answers the question: "Has this ownership been earned at all?"

Vesting answers the question: "Of the ownership already earned, how much is no longer subject to repurchase?"

This means:

- unearned equity is not owned
- earned equity can still be unvested
- earned but unvested equity remains subject to repurchase at the original acquisition price

### 4.2 Vesting Terms

The proposed time-based vesting terms are:

- vesting commencement date: the effective date of the signed founder agreement, unless final legal documents specify another date
- vesting term: `48 months`
- cliff: `12 months`
- at month `12`, `25%` of each then-earned tranche vests
- after month `12`, the remaining `75%` of each earned tranche vests in equal monthly installments over the next `36` months

This is a standard founder vesting structure designed to balance founder protection with company protection.

### 4.3 How Vesting Applies to Earned Tranches

Each stage-based ownership tranche must first be earned before it transfers. Once a tranche is earned, its vested portion is measured using the common vesting timeline from the vesting commencement date.

This means a later-earned tranche can already be partially vested at the moment it is earned, because vesting is measured against the shared company timeline rather than starting a separate vesting clock for each stage.

Example:

- if the Founder earns the first `20%` tranche at month `6`, none of it is vested until month `12`
- at month `12`, `25%` of that tranche becomes vested, which means `5%` of company ownership is vested
- if the Founder earns the later `25%` tranche at month `18`, then `37.5%` of that tranche is already vested at that time under the common vesting schedule, which equals `9.375%` vested and `15.625%` unvested

The same logic applies to the Co-founder's earned tranches.

### 4.4 Unearned Equity

Any equity tied to an incomplete stage remains unearned and does not transfer. It does not become owned by time alone, by title, or by partial effort unless both founders specifically agree otherwise in writing.

Unearned equity:

- stays outside the departing founder's ownership
- remains available to Halava for future allocation only by written agreement
- does not automatically move to the other founder

### 4.5 Repurchase Right

If a founder leaves while holding earned but unvested equity, Halava may repurchase that unvested equity at the original acquisition price.

Proposed repurchase mechanics:

- exercise window: within `90` days after departure, default, or other defined trigger
- price: original acquisition price, not fair market value
- scope: earned but unvested equity only, unless final legal documents later define a specific bad-leaver remedy

If Halava does not exercise the repurchase right, including where the company cannot fund a buyback, the non-departing founder or another approved assignee may exercise the same repurchase right on the same terms. This is intended to prevent a situation where the company loses practical control simply because it lacks cash for a buyback.

### 4.6 Good Leaver and Bad Leaver Framework

The working good-leaver / bad-leaver framework is:

Good leaver examples:

- death or permanent disability
- removal without cause
- mutual written agreement
- inability to continue due to immigration or work-permission failure despite good-faith compliance effort

Bad leaver examples:

- fraud, theft, or intentional misconduct
- material breach of founder obligations
- abandonment of role without reasonable notice
- undisclosed conflict causing material harm
- refusal to transfer company property, accounts, code, or records

Final bad-leaver remedies should be defined in the signed legal documents. The guiding principle is that no founder should lose already vested equity except under a clearly defined and signed misconduct-based rule.

### 4.7 Practical Outcome on Departure

On departure:

- unearned equity never transfers
- earned and vested equity is generally retained, subject to final signed bad-leaver provisions
- earned but unvested equity may be repurchased under the repurchase rules
- operational handover remains mandatory regardless of vesting outcome

## 5. Amendments and Review

This document should be reviewed at each earn-in stage and any time there is a material change to ownership, scope, funding, authority, or founder compensation.

Any change to:

- ownership split
- stage definitions
- capital commitment
- salary terms
- IP treatment
- vesting mechanics
- decision-making rules

must be made in writing and approved by both founders.
