## 1. Assignment Rule

All product, code, design, documentation, brand assets, process documentation, business materials, data models, and operational materials created for Halava must be assigned to Halava.

This includes:

- source code and repositories
- product specifications and wireframes
- data models and architecture notes
- deployment and infrastructure configuration
- names, marks, logos, and visual assets
- merchant onboarding materials
- sales, pricing, and operations materials

---

## 3. Delivery and Control

- All Halava repositories, domains, cloud accounts, analytics tools, payment tools, and operational systems must be accessible by Halava.
- No founder may keep sole control over a critical company account without documented backup access.
- On departure, all company credentials, records, code, and devices holding company materials must be returned or transferred immediately.

---

## 4. Third-Party and Open-Source Materials

- Any open-source or third-party dependency used in Halava should be documented to the extent practical.
- No founder may introduce licensed third-party materials that create ongoing payment or legal exposure without disclosure.
- Any proprietary asset used from a founder's outside business must be separately documented and approved before use.

---

## 5. Confidential Information

- Customer lists
- merchant pipeline and business contacts
- financial information
- product plans
- source code
- security credentials

All of the above are Halava confidential information and should be protected accordingly.
