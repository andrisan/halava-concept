## 1. Purpose

This document sets out the proposed legal-form decision and governance rules for Halava.

---

## 2. Legal Form Decision

- Halava legal form is open and should be finalized as either `KK` or `GK` before incorporation.
- The founders should document the selected legal form in writing before filing incorporation.

---

## 3. Ownership

- Initial ownership holders should be the Founder and the Co-founder if legally feasible at formation under the selected legal form.
- Target ownership after full earn-in is `Founder 55%` and `Co-founder 45%`.
- Ownership is earned under [[milestone-earn-in-schedule]] and remains subject to [[vesting-and-repurchase-terms]].

---

## 4. Capital Contributions

- Stage `1` capital commitment is intended as the formation and build contribution.
- Stage `2` and `3` capital commitments are intended as additional founder funding for operations and growth.
- Each additional contribution must be documented by written founder approval before funding.
- Unless later agreed otherwise in writing, staged founder funding tied to the earn-in schedule is treated as founder capital contribution for Halava and is not automatically repayable.

---

## 5. Authority Structure

- The Co-founder may act as the initial legal representative under the selected legal form for incorporation and early operations.
- The Founder should receive the appropriate authority role once long-term operating control transitions to the Founder.
- Day-to-day operating authority should match actual role responsibility as much as legally possible.

Authority roles by legal form:

- If `KK` is selected: `Director` and `Representative Director`
- If `GK` is selected: `Member`, `Managing Member`, and `Representative Member`

---

## 6. Founder Approval Rules

The following matters should require written approval by both founders:

- admission of any new owner or member
- transfer of any ownership interest
- new equity or ownership issuance
- capital structure changes
- appointment or removal of core legal representatives under the selected legal form
- founder compensation changes
- debt above the agreed threshold
- sale of the company or major assets
- related-party transactions

---

## 7. Transfer Restrictions

- No founder may transfer, assign, pledge, or otherwise dispose of any ownership interest without written approval from the other founder and compliance with final legal documents.
- Any transfer on departure must respect the vesting and repurchase rules.
- Any third-party admission must be documented by founder approval before completion.

---

## 8. Distributions

- Profit distributions, if any, should follow ownership percentages unless both founders agree otherwise in writing.
- No founder may unilaterally take distributions, loans, or extraordinary reimbursements from Halava.

---

## 9. Related-Party Rules

- Any contract, service arrangement, channel arrangement, referral arrangement, or funding arrangement involving a founder's outside business interests must be disclosed in advance.
- Any related-party transaction requires prior written approval by both founders.
- Transaction terms should be commercially reasonable and documented.

---

## 10. Records and Reporting

- Halava should maintain a current cap table or ownership ledger.
- Halava should maintain written records of milestone sign-off, capital funding, founder approvals, and authority changes.
- Both founders should have access to company financial records, major contracts, and core operating information.
