## 1. Core Rule

- Unearned equity is not owned.
- Earned equity is subject to time-based vesting.
- Earned but unvested equity is subject to repurchase at the original acquisition price.

---

## 2. Vesting Schedule

- Vesting commencement date: the effective date of the signed founder agreement, unless the final signed documents specify another date.
- Vesting term: `48 months`
- Cliff: no vested equity before month `12`
- At month `12`, `25%` of each then-earned tranche becomes vested.
- After month `12`, the remaining `75%` of each earned tranche vests in equal monthly installments over the next `36` months.

### Treatment of Later-Earned Tranches

- A tranche does not transfer until the related earn-in stage is completed and signed off.
- Once a tranche is earned, the vested portion of that tranche is calculated based on the common vesting schedule measured from the vesting commencement date.

This means a tranche earned later may already include a partially vested portion if enough time has passed under the common vesting schedule.

---

## 3. Unearned Equity

- Any equity tied to an uncompleted earn-in stage is not earned and does not transfer.
- Unearned equity remains available to Halava for future reallocation only by written agreement.
- Unearned equity does not automatically transfer to the other founder.

---

## 4. Repurchase Right

- Any earned but unvested equity remains subject to repurchase at the original acquisition price.
- The repurchase right may be exercised within `90` days after the founder's departure, default, or other triggering event defined in the final signed documents.
- If Halava does not exercise the repurchase right, the non-departing founder or a designated assignee approved in writing may exercise the same repurchase right on the same terms.

This is intended to avoid a situation where Halava cannot afford a fair-value buyback.

---

## 5. Good Leaver and Bad Leaver Working Draft

### Good Leaver

A founder should generally be treated as a good leaver if departure results from:

- death or permanent disability
- removal without cause
- mutual written agreement
- inability to continue due to immigration or work-permission failure despite good-faith compliance effort

### Bad Leaver

A founder should generally be treated as a bad leaver if departure results from:

- fraud, theft, or intentional misconduct
- material breach of founder obligations
- abandonment of role without notice
- undisclosed conflict causing material harm
- refusal to transfer company property, accounts, code, or records

Final bad-leaver remedies should be defined in signed legal documents.

---

## 6. Practical Rules

- Vesting applies separately from earn-in. A stage must first be earned before vesting matters.
- No founder should receive unearned equity by time alone.
- No founder should lose already vested equity except under a clearly signed bad-leaver or misconduct provision.
