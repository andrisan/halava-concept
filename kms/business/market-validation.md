## 1. Primary Research Summary

### 1.1 Consumer Research

**Target segment:** Muslim consumers in Japan (residents, students, workers, tourists)

#### Pain Points Identified

| Pain Point                                | Severity | Frequency    |
| ----------------------------------------- | -------- | ------------ |
| Difficulty finding trusted halal products | High     | Daily/Weekly |
| High delivery fees for small orders       | High     | Per purchase |
| No coordination tools for group purchases | Medium   | Weekly       |
| Lack of price comparison                  | Medium   | Per purchase |

### 1.2 Merchant Research

**Target segment:** Halal shops, restaurants, and grocers in Japan

#### Operational Challenges Identified

| Challenge | Impact | Current Solution |
|-----------|--------|------------------|
| No unified online presence | Lost sales | Instagram, LINE, WhatsApp |
| Manual shipment management | Time-consuming | Kuroneko/Sagawa direct |
| No tools for bulk orders | Missed revenue | Ad-hoc via phone |
| Separate systems for POS/online | Inventory errors | Manual reconciliation |
| High commission fees | Margin pressure | 30-35% to UberEats/Wolt |

## 2. Market Size & Expansion Strategy

### 2.1 TAM — Total Addressable Market (Global)

A massive, globally underserved market:

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Global halal market** | Multi-trillion dollar industry (food, cosmetics, lifestyle) |
| **Global Muslim population** | 1.8+ billion + health-conscious consumers |
| **Growth trajectory** | One of the fastest-growing consumer segments globally |
| **Halal e-commerce penetration** | Very low (massive opportunity) |

### 2.2 SAM — Serviceable Available Market (Multi-Tier Approach)

#### Tier 1 — Muslim-Minority Markets (Halal trust is primary pain)

| Region | Markets | Characteristics |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **East Asia** | Japan, South Korea, Taiwan | Growing Muslim tourism + diaspora |
| **Singapore** | Singapore | Multicultural, wealthy, English-speaking hub |
| **Europe** | UK, France, Germany | Large established Muslim populations |
| **North America** | USA, Canada | Diaspora + halal-curious consumers |

**Combined market size:** Tens of billions in halal commerce (high willingness to pay for trust)

#### Tier 2 — Muslim-Majority Markets (Merchant tools + group purchase primary value)

| Region | Markets | Characteristics |
|--------|---------|-----------------|
| **Southeast Asia** | Indonesia, Malaysia | 270M+ Muslims, rapidly growing e-commerce, halal hub for SEA |
| **South Asia** | Pakistan, Bangladesh | 350M+ Muslims, rapidly growing e-commerce |
| **Middle East** | Egypt, Turkey | Large populations, cross-border commerce |
| **GCC** | UAE, Saudi Arabia | Wealthy, tech-savvy consumers |

**Combined market size:** Hundreds of billions in halal commerce (massive volume, lower fees acceptable)

### 2.3 SOM — Serviceable Obtainable Market (Japan Launch)

**Why Japan first:**
- Established B2B halal supplier network
- Regulatory clarity
- Halal trust is acute pain for consumers
- Indonesian diaspora as ideal early adopter segment

#### Primary Target: Indonesian Diaspora in Japan

**Source: Embassy of Indonesia, 2026**

| Metric | Value |
|--------|-------|
| **Indonesian nationals in Japan** | 230,689 |
| **Year-over-year growth** | 37.2% |
| **Growth trajectory** | Fast-growing foreign community |

**Why Indonesian diaspora first:**
- Largest Muslim nationality group in Japan
- Strong halal dietary observance
- Active community networks (mosques, student associations, worker groups)
- High digital adoption and smartphone usage
- Underserved by existing Japanese e-commerce platforms

#### Broader Muslim Population in Japan

| Segment | Estimated Population |
|---------|---------------------|
| **Indonesian residents** | 230,689 |
| **Other Muslim residents** | 70,000-100,000 (estimate) |
| **Students (all nationalities)** | 30,000-50,000 (estimate) |
| **Tourists (annual)** | 1.5-2M pre-pandemic (estimate) |

#### Japan Launch Milestones

| Stage | Target | Key Metrics |
|-------|--------|-------------|
| **Stage 1 (Validation)** | 5–10 pilot merchants | MVP live, honor-based fee validation |
| **Stage 2 (Traction)** | 15–25 active merchants | ¥5M+ monthly GMV |
| **Stage 3 (Commitment)** | 50+ merchants | ¥10M+ monthly GMV, national expansion ready |

#### Japan Market Expansion Sequence

Within Japan, adoption follows a deliberate sequence by community:

1. **Indonesian community** — Largest Muslim group (230k+), strong networks, ideal early adopters
2. **Other Southeast Asian communities** — Malaysian, Vietnamese Muslims
3. **South Asian communities** — Bangladeshi, Pakistani residents
4. **Other Muslim residents** — Middle Eastern, African, and other communities
5. **Muslim tourists** — Inbound tourism market (1.5-2M annually)

### 2.4 Strategic Expansion Roadmap

| Phase | Timeline | Markets | Strategy |
|-------|----------|---------|----------|
| **Prove model** | Year 1–3 | Japan only | Establish product-market fit, B2B network traction, regulatory pathway |
| **Tier 1 expansion** | Year 4–7 | Taiwan, South Korea | Geographic proximity, large Southeast Asian worker populations, similar regulatory environment |
| **Remaining Tier 1** | Year 7–9 | Singapore, UK, France, Germany, USA, Canada | Bridge positioning; strong halal infrastructure and diaspora communities |
| **Tier 2 expansion** | Year 9–12 | Indonesia, Malaysia, Pakistan, Bangladesh, UAE | Muslim-majority markets; adapt to group purchase + merchant tools, massive volume |
| **Global coverage** | Year 12+ | Turkey, Egypt, rest of Middle East | Platform effects at scale |

### 2.5 Japan Halal Market Size (Estimates)

> **Note:** The following figures are industry estimates from various secondary sources and cannot be independently verified. They are provided for directional context only.

| Metric                       | Estimated Value         |
| ---------------------------- | ----------------------- |
| Japan halal market size      | ¥200-300B ($1.5-2B USD) |
| Annual growth rate           | 8-10%                   |
| Halal e-commerce penetration | <2%                     |

**Limitations:**
- No authoritative single source for Japan halal market data
- Estimates vary widely across reports
- "Halal market" definition inconsistent (food only vs. all halal products)
- These figures should not be used for financial projections
