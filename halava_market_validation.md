## 1. Primary Research Summary

### 1.1 Consumer Research

**Target segment:** Muslim consumers in Japan (residents, students, workers, tourists)

#### Pain Points Identified

| Pain Point | Severity | Frequency | Source |
|------------|----------|-----------|--------|
| Difficulty finding trusted halal products in unfamiliar areas | High | Daily/Weekly | [Pending: Consumer survey] |
| High delivery fees for small orders | High | Per purchase | [Pending: Consumer survey] |
| No coordination tools for group purchases | Medium | Weekly | [Pending: Consumer survey] |
| Lack of price comparison across halal merchants | Medium | Per purchase | [Pending: Consumer survey] |
| Generic e-commerce platforms don't understand halal needs | High | Ongoing | [Pending: Consumer survey] |

#### Consumer Survey Template

**Survey objectives:**
- Quantify halal shopping frequency and spend
- Validate pain point severity rankings
- Measure willingness to use group purchase features
- Assess trust factors in halal certification

**Key metrics to capture:**
- [ ] Average monthly halal grocery spend (¥)
- [ ] Frequency of online vs. in-store halal purchases
- [ ] Top 3 pain points when finding halal products
- [ ] Interest in group purchase coordination (1-5 scale)
- [ ] Trust factors: certification vs. merchant declaration

**Target sample:** 50-100 Muslim consumers in Tokyo/Osaka metropolitan areas

### 1.2 Merchant Research

**Target segment:** Halal shops, restaurants, and grocers in Japan

#### Operational Challenges Identified

| Challenge | Impact | Current Solution | Source |
|-----------|--------|------------------|--------|
| No unified online presence | Lost sales | Instagram, LINE, WhatsApp | [Pending: Merchant interviews] |
| Manual shipment management | Time-consuming | Kuroneko/Sagawa direct | [Pending: Merchant interviews] |
| No tools for coordinating bulk orders | Missed revenue | Ad-hoc via phone/messaging | [Pending: Merchant interviews] |
| Separate systems for POS and online orders | Inventory errors | Manual reconciliation | [Pending: Merchant interviews] |
| High commission fees on delivery platforms | Margin pressure | 30-35% to UberEats/Wolt | [Pending: Merchant interviews] |

#### Merchant Interview Template

**Interview objectives:**
- Understand current sales channels and pain points
- Validate capability-based model appeal
- Assess willingness to adopt new platform
- Identify fee sensitivity thresholds

**Key questions:**
1. What percentage of sales are online vs. in-store?
2. What tools do you currently use for order management?
3. How do you handle bulk/group orders today?
4. What commission rate would you consider acceptable?
5. Would you use an integrated POS + online system?

**Target sample:** 15-25 halal merchants across shop types (grocery, restaurant, hybrid)

### 1.3 Competitor User Reviews Analysis

**Platforms analyzed:**
- BASE (Japan)
- STORES.jp
- Rakuten Ichiba
- UberEats Japan
- Existing halal-specific apps (directory only)

#### Competitor Strengths & Weaknesses

| Platform | Strengths | Weaknesses | Opportunity for Halava |
|----------|-----------|------------|------------------------|
| **BASE** | Simple setup, low fees (3.6%) | No halal focus, no POS integration | Halal trust + unified POS |
| **STORES** | Easy storefront creation | Higher fees (5%), no group purchase | Group purchase + lower effective fees |
| **Rakuten** | Large user base, trust | High setup fees (¥50k+), complex | Low barrier entry, capability-based |
| **UberEats** | Delivery logistics handled | 30-35% commission, no ownership | Merchant-owned storefront, low fees |
| **Halal apps** | Halal-focused directory | Directory only, no commerce | Full commerce + discovery |
